About Tuzla

Tuzla is one of the oldest settlements in Europe with a continuity of living. The remains of an old settlement of the Sojenič type from the Neolithic period were also evidenced. Archaeologists have discovered numerous settlements with rich remains of the material culture of the ancient inhabitants of this area. A large number of Neolithic clay pots were found with various ornaments of black, gray and red ceramics as well as stone blocks, axes, scrapers and others. Remains of food, human bones, animal bones, and various fruits that used to feed people of that age were discovered. And these data confirmed the claims that the area of ​​Tuzla was inhabited even in the younger stone age. Among the archaeological finds found,a ceramic neolithic bowl was used, which was intended for cooking salty water and producing salt. The first written mention of Tuzla dates from 950."On Governance of the State" explicitly mentioned Tuzla as a city, under the Roman name Salines meaning the town of salt, with the reminder that it is part of Raška knez Časlava.

The crime on Kapija

The massacre at Kapija, also known as 'The Crime of Tuzla's Youth', took place on May 25, 1995 in Tuzla. At 20: 55h, Ozren's Chetnik-Serbian hordes fired a grenade at the Kapija promenade in downtown Tuzla, killing 71 people and wound more than 200 people. The youngest victim was only 3 years old. Up to now, the officer of Republika Srpska Army, Novak Đukić, was sentenced to 25 years in prison for a crime before the first instance court. The biggest square in Bosnia and Herzegovina has opened

In Tuzla on 7th August 2009 the Freedom Square, which is also the largest square in BiH, is officially opened. Realization of this project is an important step in the history of Tuzla. In early August 2008, the Municipality of Tuzla started implementing a project for the design of the oldest square in the city and the promenade area of about 6,000 square meters, for which two and a half million marks were allocated. The dominant place on the square occupies a large fountain whose exterior walls are decorated with ornaments of stećak, but that on the Square of Freedom will not be the only symbols of medieval Bosnia. Namely, on the part where the oldest green area was preserved, and the old fountain until recently was erected a statue symbolizing the stećak, and the epitaphs of medieval tombstones were engraved on the marble slabs where the site was coated. It is planned to build a town house on the square in the next period on the foundation.
